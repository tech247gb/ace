Topic: Addressing the head, hand and heart with experiential learning

Agile becomes transformational when it moves from doing agile (practices) to being agile (principles) and ultimately to continuously becoming agile (enablers). Learning is transformational when it moves from learning by listening (head), to learning by doing (head, heart), and ultimately, to learning by co-creating (head, heart, hand). Both require addressing the whole system; broadening beyond the individual, to the team, the organization, and ultimately the entire ecosystem.

Todayâ€™s agile learning systems have a blind spot. They do not address the whole person by being limited to technical/practices (head) and reflective/principles (head, hand); they do not address the whole system (individual, team, organization AND ecosystem). In this presentation we will discuss an experiential learning system that connects head, hand and heart; that enables new agile ways of working through co-creation; and connect people within and across teams and organizations into an ecosystem based on shared experience.

Biography:

I am a founder of Okaloa. As a creator of Okaloa Flowlab, I teach and coach agile thinking (before methods) by making use of business simulations. I help organizations to develop flow, collaboration and learning capabilities across all functions. I use systems thinking and other thinking models to develop a deeper understanding of how work works and to develop the foundations of upstream, customer and portfolio kanban to show how flow, collaboration and learning apply at scale. My current focus is on creating an open co-operative network of skilled Okaloa facilitators that can help organizations transform. I am a regular speaker at international conferences and recipient of the 2015 Brickell Key award for outstanding contribution to the Kanban community.
